Meta-Challenges:

The biggest “hump” I have at the moment is a growing realisation that I have some OCD. Its very subtle and probably is not something that would need to be attended to unless I was under pressure (e.g. in this learning environment). The issue I have has been with me since I was a child and is a compulsion to be able to keep my learning and work “under control”. It manifests as work-a-holism, being unable to keep unread books in my house (they taunt me with their undigested knowledge) and the constant feeling that I am failing and don't have enough time. I still have the occasional nightmare about getting behind on maths class in school, or not doing my homework.. which seems altogether ridiculous at age 39.

I also struggle not to do things sequentially, and to not finish things. I want to do things end-to-end because I feel like this is lazy or whatever. But many things should be given up, retired or put to bed. They are not possible with children, or indeed modern life. The fact that I can still speak Mandarin and Italian ten years after having studied them shows you how much I am willing to punish myself in order not to lose my edge. I’m proud of these achievements but they cost me a lot of happiness - I always feel like I am not doing enough and scared to undertake new skills or challenges because of the burden of continuing them. My all or nothing somewhat obsessive nature isn't very forgiving on a human with only 24 hours in the day.

I want my learning to be carefree, not just another way to self flagellate and punish myself. I feel that my OCD is very harsh with me, as my eating disorder used to be. It doesn't allow for grace and is not congruent with a growth mentality and mindset.

I hope that I can work on this during my time at Dev Academy, but I don't really know what the solution is. I guess you just have to practise letting go until it becomes a habit.
